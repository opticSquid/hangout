## Guiding the [[End User Experience]]
The **[[End User Experience]]** encompasses the entire journey, from the moment an **[[End User]]** opens the app until they successfully avail a service from a **[[Platform Partner]]**, and every interaction in between. Our design principles are centered around making this journey as intuitive and efficient as possible.
1. **Streamlined Interactions:** Every step in the user's journey should be meaningful, respecting their time and effort. Useless or unrelated steps will be eliminated to ensure a fluid experience.
2. **Clear Navigation:** Users should be seamlessly guided from one step to the next, with proper guidance and "hand-holding" provided whenever necessary to prevent confusion.
3. **Unified Pathways:** To ensure both ease of debugging for us and a less confusing experience for **[[End User]]s**, there will be only one primary way to achieve a particular state or outcome within the app.
4. **Mobile-First & Gesture-Driven:** The app will be designed primarily for mobile platforms and will heavily rely on swipe-based gestures for intuitive navigation.
5. **Short Video Content Focus:** The app will exclusively support short video content in a vertical 16:9 aspect ratio, optimized for mobile viewing.
## The [[End User]] Journey
Here's a breakdown of the typical **[[End User]]** journey within the app
1. Onboarding & Discovery
	1. The journey begins with a brief pop-over explaining what Hangout is and its core capabilities in simple, understandable terms.
	2. A guided tour will then introduce the high-level features of the app, allowing users to discover other functionalities as they explore.
	3. If a user hasn't signed up yet, they'll be shown diverse content from nearby **[[Platform Partner]]s** to pique their interest.
2. Personalization & Recommendations
	1. Upon signing up, the user will be prompted to provide their information and general preferences.
	2. Based on this initial data, the app will start recommending content. As users view and interact with more content, the app will gather additional data about their preferences, further personalizing future recommendations. [[Personalization]]
3. Content Interaction & Service Booking
	1. For individual posts, users will see options to heart, comment, and share. Location information of the [[Platform Partner]] will be visible, along with a button to book the service directly.
	2. The location information will clearly display both the **[[Platform Partner]]'s** location and the user's own location.
4. Post-Booking Experience
	1. Once a user books a service from a **[[Platform Partner]]** and completes the payment process (see [[Simplifying Checkout]]), the app will display the optimal route from their current location to the **[[Platform Partner]]'s** venue.
	2. If the user closes the app after checkout and returns later, their future and past bookings will be accessible under the **[[Purchase History]]** section of their profile.
	3. Only routes for most near future of all the upcoming orders will be visible from the **[[End User]]'s** current location, ensuring relevance and clarity.
